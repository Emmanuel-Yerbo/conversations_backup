### 🤖 Gemini/Antigravity — *2026-06-26 02:24:50*

In academic and scientific writing, using **"we"** (known as the *academic we* or *authorial plural*) is a standard convention, even for sole-authored papers. 

There are two main reasons for this:
1. **Objective Distance:** It shifts the focus away from the individual author and toward the research itself, making the writing sound more objective and less conversational.
2. **Engaging the Reader:** It is used to invite the reader along in the research process (e.g., *"If we look at Figure 1..."* or *"We find that..."* means "The reader and I together observe that...").

Using **"I"** or **"me"** is generally discouraged in scientific papers because it can sound too informal or subjective to reviewers. 

### Best Solution for a Sole Author:
If you do not want to use "we" (since you are the only author), the standard practice is to use **passive voice** instead. This avoids both "we" and "I". 

For example:
*   Instead of: *"We trained the network..."*
*   You write: *"The network was trained..."*
