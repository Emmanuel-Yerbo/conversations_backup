The standard formula for the **Nitrogen Sufficiency Index (NSI)** is:

$$\text{NSI} = \frac{\text{VI}_{\text{target}}}{\text{VI}_{\text{reference}}}$$

Where:
*   **$\text{VI}_{\text{target}}$**: The Vegetation Index value (e.g., NDRE, NDVI, or SPAD chlorophyll reading) measured at the target location (the zone being assessed).
*   **$\text{VI}_{\text{reference}}$**: The Vegetation Index value of a highly fertilized reference strip (known as an **"N-rich strip"**), representing a crop zone with non-limiting nitrogen where maximum greenness and chlorophyll density have been achieved.

### Key Interpretation Values
*   **$\text{NSI} \ge 0.95$**: The crop is considered **sufficient**. Adding more nitrogen will not increase yield (it results in "luxury consumption" and environmental runoff).
*   **$\text{NSI} < 0.95$**: The crop is **deficient**. It requires additional nitrogen fertilizer, with the prescription rate scaled to the severity of the deficit.
