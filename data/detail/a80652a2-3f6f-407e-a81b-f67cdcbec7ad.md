Here is the explanation in simple, plain English:

### 1. The difference between "Words" and "Characters"
*   **A Word** is a full group of letters separated by spaces (for example: `"Google"` is 1 word, `"Earth Engine"` is 2 words).
*   **A Character** is every single individual key you press on your keyboard. This includes every letter, number, punctuation mark, and **even the spaces** between words. 
    *   For example, the word `"GIS"` is **1 word**, but it is **3 characters** (`G` + `I` + `S`).
    *   The phrase `"GIS is fun."` is **3 words**, but it is **11 characters** (including the spaces and the period).

---

### 2. What `0/10000` means on SurveyHeart
When a website shows `0/10000` under a text box, it is counting **Characters (letters & spaces)**, not words. 
*   If you type one letter (like `A`), the counter will say `1/10000`.
*   If you type a space, it will say `2/10000`.
